## Overview

SketchUp Pro Advanced is an intuitive 3D modeling application from Trimble known for its unique push-pull modeling approach that makes 3D design accessible to architects, interior designers, woodworkers, landscape designers, and anyone who needs to visualize ideas in three dimensions without the steep learning curve of traditional CAD. It has democratized 3D modeling by proving that powerful 3D tools can be genuinely easy to learn.

Push-pull modeling creates 3D geometry by drawing 2D shapes and pushing or pulling them into three-dimensional forms, making the fundamental 3D modeling operation intuitive rather than abstract. The approach mirrors how people naturally think about 3D shapes — draw a rectangle, push it up to make a box, push a circle into the box to make a hole. This intuitive workflow makes SketchUp learnable in hours rather than weeks.

3D Warehouse provides millions of free, community-created 3D models that can be downloaded and placed directly into designs — furniture, fixtures, vehicles, vegetation, architectural elements — eliminating the need to model common objects from scratch. The warehouse transforms SketchUp from a modeler into a 3D composition tool where complex scenes are assembled from existing components.

Layout produces professional 2D documentation from 3D models with dimensioning, annotation, and page layout tools that generate construction drawings, presentations, and documentation directly from the model. The documentation maintains live links to the model, updating automatically when the design changes.

For architects, designers, and creators who want to work in 3D without the complexity of traditional CAD, SketchUp Pro Advanced is the modeling tool that makes 3D design as natural as sketching on paper.

## Performance

SketchUp Pro balances performance with system resource usage intelligently. Operations are handled in the background, keeping your system responsive for everything else. Efficient memory management and smart caching reduce overhead during extended work sessions.

Apple Silicon acceleration is fully utilized on M-series chips, delivering noticeably faster performance. On Intel machines, hardware acceleration is used wherever available.
